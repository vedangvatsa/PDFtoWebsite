# CVin.Bio — Social Media Content & Image Design Guidelines

These guidelines dictate the strict content strategy, visual design standards, and platform-specific rules for all social media posts distributed across LinkedIn, X, Bluesky, Facebook, Instagram, Dev.to, Hashnode, and Tumblr.

---

## 1. Content Philosophy

### Voice & Persona
- **Tone**: Grounded, sharp, direct. Never preachy. Never corporate.
- **Perspective**: An insider who understands the broken job market. Someone who has been on both sides. Not selling. Just stating facts and offering an alternative.
- **Reader**: A mid-career professional (25-40) who is quietly frustrated with the hiring system. They recognize broken patterns. They respond to blunt truths.

### Absolute Content Rules
- **Zero colon introductions.** Do not use `:` to lead into lists or thoughts. Use hard periods.
- **Zero em-dashes or en-dashes.** Break thoughts into short, complete sentences.
- **Zero AI slop.** No "In today's digital landscape." No "leverage your potential." No "unlock opportunities." Write like a human who has been in the room.
- **Zero hashtag spam.** Maximum 3 hashtags per post. Only on platforms that use them (LinkedIn, Instagram, X). Never more.
- **Zero emojis in body text.** Emojis are acceptable only in the very last line as a visual anchor. Never scatter them through paragraphs.
- **CTA is always the last line.** Every post ends with the URL `cvin.bio`. No "Sign up now!" or "Click here!" The URL speaks for itself.

### Content Structure (Per Post)
1. **Hook** (Line 1-2): A blunt fact, screenshot description, statistic, or relatable frustration. This must stop the scroll.
2. **Body** (Lines 3-8): The observation, story, or argument. Short sentences. Hard line breaks between thoughts. Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph block.
3. **Pivot** (Line 9-10): A single sentence that shifts from the problem to the solution. This is the bridge.
4. **CTA** (Final line): `cvin.bio` — standalone, no decoration.

### Content Categories
| Category | Description | Ratio |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Frustration Posts** | Real stories from Reddit, Blind, or common experience. "They ghosted after 4 rounds." | 40% |
| **Stat Posts** | Hard numbers about the job market, ATS rejection rates, recruiter behavior. | 25% |
| **Comparison Posts** | PDF vs Link. Old way vs New way. Before vs After. | 20% |
| **Infographic Posts** | Visual data. Bar charts, pie charts, timelines. | 15% |

## 2. Image Design Standards

### Universal Design Rules
Every social media image must follow the CVin.Bio brand identity strictly.

- **Background**: Pure stark white (`#FFFFFF`) or deep black (`#09090B`). No gradients. No textures. No stock photography backgrounds.
- **Typography**: Inter (Bold 700-800 for headlines, Regular 400 for body). If Inter is unavailable, use any tight geometric sans-serif.
- **Letter Spacing**: Headlines use extremely tight kerning (`-0.04em` to `-0.06em`). Body text uses standard kerning.
- **Color Palette**:
  - Primary text: `#09090B` (on white) or `#FAFAFA` (on dark)
  - Muted text: `#71717A`
  - Accent (sparingly): `#6366F1` (indigo-500) — only for data highlights, never for backgrounds
  - Never use red, orange, or neon colors. Never.
- **Layout**: Mathematically rigid. Center-aligned or strict left-aligned. Generous whitespace. No cluttered compositions. Every element must sit on a visible grid.
- **No stock photos.** No generic "person at laptop" imagery. No handshake photos. No skylines.
- **No device frames.** Do not wrap UI screenshots in laptop or phone mockups unless explicitly requested.

### Image Dimensions Per Platform
| Platform | Aspect Ratio | Pixels | Notes |
|----------|-------------|--------|-------|
| LinkedIn | 1.91:1 | 1200 × 628 | Landscape. Most important. |
| Instagram | 1:1 | 1080 × 1080 | Square. Text must be large enough to read on mobile. |
| X (Twitter) | 16:9 | 1600 × 900 | Landscape. Similar to LinkedIn but wider. |
| Facebook | 1.91:1 | 1200 × 628 | Same as LinkedIn. |
| Bluesky | 16:9 | 1600 × 900 | Same as X. |

### Image Types

#### Type 1: Text-Only Cards (For Frustration & Stat Posts)
- White background, centered text block.
- Headline in bold black, 48-72px equivalent.
- Supporting stat or quote in muted gray, 24-32px.
- `cvin.bio` watermark bottom-right, 14px, muted gray.
- No borders. No shadows. No decorative elements.

#### Type 2: Data Visualizations (For Infographic Posts)
- Minimal bar charts, pie charts, or comparison tables.
- Use only black, muted gray, and one accent color (indigo).
- Labels inside or directly adjacent to data. No separate legends.
- Chart must be readable without squinting on a phone screen.

#### Type 3: Comparison Cards (For PDF vs Link Posts)
- Two-column or two-row layout.
- Left/Top = "Old Way" (muted, crossed out, gray tones).
- Right/Bottom = "New Way" (clean, bold, slight indigo accent).
- Visual weight must clearly favor the "New Way" column.

#### Type 4: Screenshot/Quote Cards
- When referencing a Reddit post or real scenario, simulate a clean text excerpt.
- No actual Reddit UI screenshots (copyright risk).
- Style as a minimal blockquote with light left border in indigo.

### Logo & Branding Placement
- The `cvin.bio` text mark (not a logo graphic) appears in every image.
- Position: bottom-right corner, 14-16px, color `#71717A`.
- Never make the brand mark the dominant visual element. It's a watermark.

## 3. Platform-Specific Adaptations

### LinkedIn
- Maximum 3,000 characters. Use all of them when the story warrants it.
- First 2 lines must hook. LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters with "...see more".
- Line breaks between every sentence or every 2 sentences. Dense paragraphs get zero engagement.
- End with 2-3 relevant hashtags. Example: `#careers #jobsearch #hiring`

### X (Twitter)
- Maximum 280 characters. Posts must be punchy and complete within the limit.
- No hashtags unless they add meaning. Space is too precious.
- Image does the heavy lifting on X. The text is the hook.

### Bluesky
- Maximum 300 characters. Similar to X but slightly more room.
- No hashtags. Bluesky culture rejects them.
- Clean, factual, no marketing language.

### Instagram
- Caption can be long but the image must stand alone.
- Use square (1:1) images. Text on image must be readable at phone scale (minimum 36px effective).
- Maximum 5 hashtags in the first comment, not in the caption.

### Facebook
- Similar to LinkedIn but more casual tone acceptable.
- Image is critical for feed algorithm. Always attach one.
- Engagement bait is penalized. Never say "Tag someone who needs this."

### Dev.to / Hashnode
- Longer form. These are developer community platforms.
- Posts should feel like micro-articles, not marketing.
- Include code snippets, technical observations, or system-level analysis when possible.
- Cover image uses 16:9 (1600 × 900).

### Tumblr
- Visual-first platform. The image is primary content.
- Text posts work but must be formatted with markdown.
- Tags go at the bottom, not in the body text.

---

## 4. Scheduling & Cadence

- **3 posts per day** across all platforms.
- **Posting times (IST)**: 01:00, 09:00, 17:00
- **No duplicate content** across platforms on the same day. Each slot gets unique text appropriate to the platform's character limits and culture.
- **Image reuse is acceptable** across platforms since audiences rarely overlap. But the text must be adapted.

## 5. AI Image Generation Prompt Template

When using an AI image generator to create social media assets, use this base prompt and modify the bracketed sections:

> Generate a social media image for a professional platform called 'CVin.Bio'. The image should be [ASPECT RATIO, e.g. 1200x628 landscape].
>
> Background: pure white (#FFFFFF). Typography: Inter or similar tight geometric sans-serif. Primary text color: deep black (#09090B). Muted secondary text: (#71717A). 
>
> The image contains: [DESCRIBE THE CONTENT — e.g. "A centered statistic reading '72% of CVs are rejected before a human sees them' in bold 64px black text, with a smaller line below in gray reading 'Source: Harvard Business Review 2024'"].
>
> Bottom-right corner: the text 'cvin.bio' in 14px muted gray as a subtle watermark.
>
> The design must be aggressively minimal. No gradients, no shadows, no decorative borders, no stock imagery. Pure structural typography on white. The emotional tone is premium, hyper-crisp, and authoritative.
