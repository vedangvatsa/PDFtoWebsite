# Official Article & Editorial Guidelines

These guidelines dictate the strict linguistic, structural, and philosophical rules for all blog content, marketing copy, and articles generated for this application. Any divergence from these rules is considered a critical brand failure.

## 1. Core Terminology & Product Philosophy
*   **It is a CV, never a Resume.** The product converts a "CV" into a "web link". You must aggressively avoid the word "resume" in all modern copy.
*   **The PDF is Dead.** The site does *not* sell a PDF formatting or printing service. Do not write about "printing out paper copies" or "legacy PDF downloads". The primary value proposition is instantly turning a document into a clean, mobile-friendly interactive website URL.
*   **No Hard Selling.** The brand philosophy is grounded, tasteful, and helpful. Do not use loud, aggressive Call-to-Action (CTA) language (e.g., *buy now*, *sign up instantly*). The CTA should act as a native, sophisticated tool integrated into the reading experience.

## 2. Strict Linguistic Constraints
*   **Zero Colons.** You must never use a colon (`:`) to introduce a list or separate a clause. Use natural sentence mechanics and hard periods instead.
*   **Zero Em-Dashes.** You must never use em-dashes (`—`) or en-dashes (`–`). Break thoughts into distinct, complete, short sentences.
*   **Zero AI "Slop".** Aggressively purge generic corporate adjectives, grandiose hyperbole, and repetitive filler text. Do not use phrases like "highly motivated," "synergistic," "dynamic," or "In today's fast-paced digital world." Treat the reader like a highly intelligent, busy engineer.

## 3. Structural & Formatting Requirements
*   **The 700+ Word SEO Mandate.** Every published article must significantly exceed 700 words to establish profound domain authority and capture long-tail algorithmic search traffic. 
*   **Eradicating the Wall of Text.** Human eyes natively recoil from massive, unbroken blocks of gray text. You must violently break up long paragraphs. 
    *   No paragraph may exceed three short sentences. 
    *   Inject frequent `<h2>` and `<h3>` subheadings.
    *   Utilize heavy structural elements such as `<ul>` unordered lists and `<callout>` blocks to engineer extreme visual breathing room.
*   **Front-Loaded Mechanics.** Design the text for the "Z-pattern" scanning behavior. Put the highest-value noun or verb at the exact front of the sentence. Assume the reader will skim the left margin.

## 4. Content Tone & Recruiter Psychology
*   **Brutal Pragmatism over Philosophy.** Do not write abstract career counseling advice. Write actionable, heavy, practical directives rooted strictly in modern enterprise recruiter psychology. 
*   **Focus on Systems and Physics.** If writing for engineers, emphasize how to calculate software velocity, scale, and algorithmic load rather than demanding theoretical "revenue impact" numbers they cannot legally access.
*   **Respect the Reader's Intelligence.** Assume the reader already understands basic job mechanics. Skip the introductory fluff. Tell them precisely how to beat an Applicant Tracking System (ATS), how to structure an OpenGraph asset, or how to survive a 30-second automated parser scan.

## 5. Visual Consistency for Article Assets
*   **Symmetry & Margins.** Blog typography and layout must strictly adhere to the overarching brand symmetric grid. Massive hero-style text (e.g., `72px`) should never crash into the bottom of a reading prose block.
*   **Image Integrity.** Featured OpenGraph thumbnails and blog photography must explicitly respect their container's aspect ratio (e.g., `aspect-video`). They must never clip or crop dynamically loaded text.

## 6. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) & Contextual FAQs
*   **Local FAQ Mapping Requirements.** Every single article must strictly contain at least three (3) highly contextual, article-specific Frequently Asked Questions injected natively into its database array (`faqs: [{question, answer}]`). Never use global, generic FAQs (e.g., "About us") inside article routes.
*   **Snippets over Philosophy.** FAQs must be explicitly structured to completely hack "People Also Ask" automated Google snippets and AI search agent summaries (ChatGPT/Perplexity). 
*   **Fact-First Answers.** Answers must instantly provide the concrete fact. Do not use transitionary filler like "That is an excellent question." Give the exact, undeniable structural response in the very first sentence to maximize Generative Engine algorithmic surfacing.

## 7. Next.js Architectural & Spacing Safeguards
*   **The `.not-prose` Shield.** When injecting custom geometry (like `<callout>` blocks or React elements) deep into standard article `.prose` wrappers, you must explicitly encapsulate those massive custom layers with the `not-prose` class. Failure to do this guarantees vicious CSS margin-collapsing rendering bugs.
*   **Flex-Gaps over Margins.** You must entirely replace ambiguous vertical spacing paradigms (like `space-y-*` or standard `mt-*` classes) across article payloads with mathematical `flex flex-col gap-*` layouts to completely lock down paragraph geometries regardless of standard nested HTML.
*   **Strictly Deterministic Crawler Rendering.** Under absolutely no circumstances should you generate random array iterations (e.g., `Math.random()` sorting on arrays like "Further Reading"). This fundamentally snaps the React Hydration sequence in two and absolutely fractures linear PageRank crawl loops for Googlebot algorithms. Internal linking logic must be infinitely stable and mathematically predictable.
